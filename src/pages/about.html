About Us

Who We Are: Tiger LINC is an organization dedicated to promoting well-being, resilience, and connection among education majors, while providing opportunities for hands-on professional development in the classroom.

Tiger LINC operates through two main efforts:

  • Tiger LINC Schools, a partnership with local public schools where select teacher candidates are trained to facilitate research-backed well-being groups to middle and high school students.
  • Tiger LINC Clubs, a welcoming space hosting mental health and resiliency workshops for College of Education students to learn and practice well-being strategies as they study to become educators.

Tiger LINC Schools

Select teacher candidates are trained in the CASEL validated SPARK © Teen Curriculum (Speak to the Potential, Ability, and Resilience inside every Kid) and, our Roadmap to Well-Being and Resiliency Program.

These teachers lead middle and high school students in a weekly program that includes interactive activities to encourage their social emotional learning while building connections amongst the participating students.

Towson University students cultivate meaningful connections with and between the high school students to encourage mutual growth, and increase emotional well-being and resilience.

Tiger LINC Clubs

Mental Health and Resiliency Workshops include:

  • Discussing challenges in the study of education
  • Exploring the emotional underpinnings of stress and practicing a variety of strategies to manage stress and cope in challenging situations
  • Building a supportive community and facilitating discussions
